Description

🔥 Get ready for a full-throttle weekend of bikes, beats, and good vibes – The Big Weekender returns to Havok Bike Park on Saturday 12th & Sunday 13th July 2025!

This two-day event offers something for everyone, with a great mix of riding and atmosphere. 

Here’s what to expect:

Dual Slalom & Mini Bike Races – Take part or watch the action unfold across two exciting competitions.

Camping Available – Spaces for both vans and tents whether you’re riding or spectating. Camping is arranged at the sign-in hut – cash payment on arrival.

Industry Village – Explore a selection of stalls from brands, bike companies, and local businesses.

Food Truck & Music – Enjoy great food and music throughout the weekend.

Competitions & Spot Prizes – Take part in challenges and be in with a chance to win a range of prizes.

Uplift will be running each day.

Spectators are welcome and do not require a ticket. Feel free to come along and enjoy the event, even if you’re not riding.

Important: All riders must read the Havok Bike Park Rules and complete the disclaimer form before arriving at the bike park.

🔞 Under 18s – Important Information

All riders under the age of 18 must have parent or guardian approval to participate in the event. Additional requirements apply based on age:

  • Under 18s – Must have a parent or guardian complete the disclaimer and give permission prior to riding. 
  • Under 14s – A parent or guardian must be on site at all times while the rider is on the trails. 
  • Under 12s – Must be accompanied by a parent or guardian who is riding with them at all times on the trails. 

These rules are in place for everyone’s safety and must be followed without exception. Please plan accordingly before booking tickets.

Whether you’re participating or supporting, it’s set to be a fantastic weekend. 

More info will be dropping on social media and updated on here so keep your eyes peeled!

📢 UPLIFT INFO

  • Helmets must be worn at all times while on the uplift truck
  • Wait only in designated areas – do not block fire roads or trails
  • Single uplift tokens available in the hut during the event

📢 CAMPING INFO

Van Camping – Quiet Zone

  • Van camping is located at the bottom of the hill in the main carpark and overflow field. 
  • This is a quiet zone due to nearby residential properties. 
  • Please keep noise to a minimum, especially in the evenings and early mornings. 
  • We kindly ask all guests to respect our neighbours and each other.

Tent Camping - Upper Area

  • Tent camping is situated further up the hill. 
  • An uplift service is available to help transport your kit – just ask! 
  • Noise restrictions are more relaxed here, but please keep volume down after 10pm to be courteous to fellow campers.

Respect the environment

  • Please take all rubbish home with you or use the bins provided on site. 
  • Help us protect Havok Hill by treating the area with care. 
  • If you are walking after dark, always use the fire road for your safety.

General Rules

  • All Havok Bike Park rules apply while you’re on site. 
  • If you have any questions, don’t hesitate to speak to Sam or Gem – we’re here to help!

About the host

HAVOK Bike Park

Calderdale

Welcome to HAVOK Bike Park HAVOK Bike Park is a purpose-built mountain biking destination nestled on the dramatic edge of the Calder Valley, spread across approximately 100 acres of steep, rolling hillside. Overlooking the awe-inspiring gorge of Cliviger, the park offers incredible panoramic views and a riding experience that’s as scenic as it is fun. Designed for riders of all abilities, HAVOK features a diverse range of trails — from flowing beginner-friendly lines to technical descents that will challenge even the most experienced riders. Whether you're here to progress your skills or push your limits, there’s something for everyone. Our dedicated dirt jump zone sits in one of the park’s most stunning spots, giving riders the chance to catch big air while soaking in some of the most amazing views in the region. Come ride, connect, and experience the energy of a bike park built by riders, for riders. As a Community Interest Company (CIC), HAVOK exists for the benefit of riders and the wider community. We're more than just a bike park — we're a hub for connection, progression, and shared passion. We’re committed to building a welcoming, inclusive, and safe space for riders of all backgrounds and abilities. At the heart of everything we do is community, craftsmanship, and care — not only for our riders but also for the land we ride on. We are dedicated to preserving the environment and respecting the wildlife that shares our space. Sustainability and stewardship guide the way we build and maintain trails, and shape the future of HAVOK. New features, trails, and improvements are being added all the time, so stay connected with us on Facebook and Instagram for the latest updates. We’re proud to be rider owned and operated, and we can’t wait to welcome you to HAVOK. PARK RULES To ensure everyone has a safe and enjoyable experience at HAVOK, please read and follow the rules below: Rider Safety 1. Protective gear must be worn at all times when riding. We strongly recommend a full-face helmet and knee pads as a minimum. 2. Full-face helmets and kneepads are mandatory for all riders under 16. 3. Under 18s must have a parent or guardian sign their disclaimer form before riding the park. 4. Under 14s must always be accompanied by a parent or guardian while riding at the park. Equipment & Conduct 6. Your bike must be in safe, working condition, with suitable components for the terrain – disc brakes and suspension are required. 7. Riding under the influence of alcohol or drugs is strictly prohibited. 8. Stay on designated trails – do not cut corners, modify features, or create unofficial lines. 9. Never ride back up the trails. Do not stop or linger on the track unless absolutely necessary (e.g., emergency or injury). 10. Ride with care and respect for others – including slower riders, walkers, and HAVOK staff. 11. Obey all trail signage and follow instructions related to maintenance, trail closures, or diversions. Fire Road & Uplift Safety 12. The fire road must be kept clear when the uplift vehicle is in operation. Do not obstruct the route at any time. 13. When using the uplift service: * A properly fitted helmet must be worn at all times, including during vehicle transit. * Follow staff instructions and behave respectfully at all times. * Unsafe or disruptive behaviour will not be tolerated and may result in removal from the park. By entering and riding at HAVOK, you agree to follow these rules and ride responsibly. They’re here to keep you, others, and the trails safe. Need help or have questions? Speak to a member of the HAVOK team on-site or email us at enquiries@havokmtb.co.uk.
